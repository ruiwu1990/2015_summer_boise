The Lapwai Creek Watershed has become a focus of cultural issues surrounding water quantity in the western U.S. Although not considered an area of significant economic impact, Lapwai Creek is encompassed almost entirely within the Nez Perce Reservation. The tribe considers both the steelhead that live and spawn in Lapwai Creek and the salmonid species in the rest of the basin sacred foods with significant value for physical sustenance and spiritual connection. The Nez Perce Treaty of 1855 guaranteed the Tribe exclusive rights to fishing within the streams running through or bordering the reserve. Often portrayed as a ‘wicked problem’ the current situation within this watershed is both complex and contentious.
